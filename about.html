About

Methodology, sources, and revision policy

What this dashboard is

A standing read of the three monthly U.S. labour-market releases — Establishment Survey (CES), Household Survey (CPS), and JOLTS — that updates each release with the same set of charts, tables, and analytical framings. The aim is to put the same headline numbers, the same sectoral breakdowns, the same alternative measures, and the same diffusion / Sahm / V-U / quits-by-industry views in front of you on jobs-Friday morning. Year to year and across cycles you can compare the same picture.

Data source

All series are pulled from Haver Analytics. There is no FRED dependency. Each rebuild fetches every series in a single round-trip and caches the result on disk; if Haver is unreachable, the dashboard rebuilds from the cached CSV.

Shutdown gap and interpolation

The September–November 2025 federal-government shutdown caused the BLS to miss the October 2025 release of the Household Survey (CPS). The Establishment Survey (CES) and JOLTS were not interrupted.

We searched Haver for shutdown-imputed variants of the affected codes (LR, LE, LF, LP, LQ, MLU5, MLU6, FA53, the unemployment-by-duration series, etc.) and none are published — the standard codes carry NaN for the missed month. The build pipeline therefore applies pandas time-based linear interpolation across interior gaps only, never extrapolating leading or trailing NaN. The interpolated month is logged in bundle_meta.json.

Revision policy

CES estimates are revised in the two months following first release and again at the annual benchmark each February. CPS is revised much less often. JOLTS levels are revised regularly — particularly the most recent two months. This dashboard always shows the latest vintage; older vintages are not preserved.
